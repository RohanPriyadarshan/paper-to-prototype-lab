# CropHealth AI - Complete Disease Detection List

Based on the research paper by **Rohan Priyadarshan** and **Saket Kumawat** from Manipal University Jaipur, this system can detect the following diseases across multiple crops with 95-99.5% accuracy.

## üçÖ TOMATO (10 Disease Classes) - 99.74% Accuracy

1. **Tomato Late Blight** (Phytophthora infestans)
   - Symptoms: Large, irregular brown spots on leaves, white fungal growth
   - Severity: High
   - Treatment: Chlorothalonil or mancozeb fungicides

2. **Tomato Early Blight** (Alternaria solani)
   - Symptoms: Dark spots with concentric rings on older leaves
   - Severity: Medium
   - Treatment: Fungicides, remove infected debris

3. **Tomato Bacterial Spot** (Xanthomonas spp.)
   - Symptoms: Small dark spots with yellow halo on leaves and fruits
   - Severity: Medium
   - Treatment: Copper-based bactericides

4. **Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot** (Septoria lycopersici)
   - Symptoms: Small circular spots with dark borders, gray centers
   - Severity: Medium
   - Treatment: Fungicides, crop rotation

5. **Tomato Target Spot** (Corynespora cassiicola)
   - Symptoms: Brown spots with concentric rings resembling target
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Treatment: Fungicide application

6. **Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus** (TYLCV)
   - Symptoms: Upward curling, yellowing of leaf margins
   - Severity: High
   - Treatment: Control whitefly vectors, resistant varieties

7. **Tomato Mosaic Virus** (ToMV)
   - Symptoms: Mottled light and dark green pattern on leaves
   - Severity: Medium-High
   - Treatment: Remove infected plants, sanitation

8. **Tomato Leaf Mold** (Passalora fulva)
   - Symptoms: Pale green/yellow spots on upper leaf, olive-brown mold below
   - Severity: Medium
   - Treatment: Improve ventilation, fungicides

9. **Tomato Spider Mites** (Tetranychus urticae)
   - Symptoms: Stippling, yellowing, fine webbing on leaves
   - Severity: Medium
   - Treatment: Miticides, biological control

10. **Tomato Healthy** (Control class)
    - No symptoms present
    - Baseline for comparison

## Usage Instructions

1. **Image Requirements:**
   - Clear photo of affected leaf
   - Good lighting conditions
   - Minimal background clutter
   - Focus on symptomatic areas

2. **Best Results:**
   - Upload high-resolution images (224√ó224 minimum)
   - Capture multiple leaves if symptoms vary
   - Include both healthy and diseased tissue
   - Avoid blurry or out-of-focus images

3. **Confidence Threshold:**
   - 95%+ confidence: Very reliable
   - 85-95% confidence: Likely accurate, monitor plant
   - Below 85%: Consider expert consultation
